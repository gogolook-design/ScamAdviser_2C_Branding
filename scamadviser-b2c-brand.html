Do

06 — Visual Language

What it looks and feels like.

06a — Photography Direction

ScamAdviser's visual storytelling reflects our mission to protect, inform, and empower users in the digital world. Our photography is authentic, human-focused, and clear. Never overly staged, fear-driven, or generic. Every image should build trust, convey expertise, and create an emotional connection with our audience.

Our User (People)

Everyday individuals of different ages, backgrounds, and locations engaging with digital life — shopping, banking, messaging, browsing. Authentic, not staged corporate stock.

Visual style
  • Natural light, soft contrast
  • Candid moments, slight movement, not overly posed
  • Clear focus on faces and body language
Keywords Authentic, diverse, relatable, engaged, empowered, informed, candid, everyday life, real people, natural light, relaxed posture, subtle smiles, gentle focus, approachable

Advisor & Scam Expert

ScamAdviser as a trusted, human expert: support staff, analysts, partners, or educators helping users or teams. Supports "Trustworthy," "Dedicated" brand traits.

Visual style
  • People at screens with dashboards, charts, or scam-related interfaces
  • Collaborative scenes: one-on-one guidance, small groups, workshops
  • Clean tech environments, but still warm and human
Keywords Trusted advisor, guidance, expert team, collaboration, explaining, showing side-by-side, mentoring, analysis, data screen, headset support, calm authority, professional but friendly

Device Interaction Close-up

Tight shots of hands and devices: tapping, scrolling, reading scam alerts, seeing trust scores. The device is the brand's stage.

Visual style
  • Simple background, strong focus on device
  • Clean, legible UI mockups, minimal glare
  • Modern devices, no outdated hardware
Keywords Modern, close-ups, interaction, clear screen, tap, scroll, notification, trust score, verification, search bar, minimal, accessible, fingerprint gestures, single-hand use

Device Usage — Indoor

Users on phones, laptops, or tablets at home or work using ScamAdviser to check a site, read an alert, or learn about scams.

Visual style
  • Warm, reassuring light
  • Simple, tidy environments (no clutter)
  • Screen content relevant (trust scores, notifications, scam articles)
Keywords Home environment, office setting, modern workspace, minimalist, natural lighting, cozy, focused, seated, desktop, laptop, kitchen table, living room, thoughtful browsing

Device Usage — Outdoor

People using ScamAdviser "on the go": in a café, park, train, street, or while traveling. The world is visible but the person and device are in focus.

Visual style
  • Natural daylight, open composition
  • The world visible but slightly blurred; device & person in focus
  • Still anchored in a digital action (checking a link, scanning a QR, etc.)
Keywords Open spaces, city street, café, park bench, public transport, travel, natural light, walking with phone, quick check, real-life scenario, community, everyday commute, accessible

Emotions

The emotional arc of scams: Before — doubt, concern, confusion. During — focus, checking, verifying with ScamAdviser. After — relief, confidence, calm empowerment.

Visual style
  • Close-ups of expressions and hands
  • Soft, calm color environments (no horror-style, no alarmist)
  • Use composition to move from "tense" to "open" (more light, more space)
Keywords Concern, doubt, focus, attention, checking, verification, confidence, relief, empowerment, calmness, reassurance, hopeful, supported, safe, composed, subtle tension → visible ease

Light & Color Treatment

Light

  • Warm natural light — window light, café glow, golden hour, lamp warmth
  • Soft directional, not flat. Shadows present but never crushed.
  • Bright but not blown out — highlights retain detail

Color Grading

  • Highlights warm-tinted toward #FFFFF8 (the brand warm white)
  • Natural skin tones, gently warm. No teal-orange Hollywood grade.
  • Shadows soft, leaning to ink #1A1A1A — never pure black
  • Overall feel: "good window in a real apartment"

Composition

Cropping & Framing

  • Tight is the default — hands, screen, face. Pick two of three.
  • Wider only when context earns it (café scene, urban street)
  • Rule of thirds for subject placement. Center only when screen content is the hero.

Depth & Space

  • Shallow depth of field for portraits — isolate subject from clutter
  • Leave 25%+ negative space for overlay (headline, score, geometric shape)
  • Negative space prefers upper-left, upper-right, or lower — subject anchors the opposite corner

Do

  • Show real people in believable situations — scrolling, tapping, reacting, deciding.
  • Use natural, soft light and clear compositions. Window light, golden hour, lamp warmth.
  • Capture calm focus and confidence, not panic. The emotional arc ends in empowerment.
  • Reflect diversity — age, gender, ethnicity, ability, location. The brand protects everyone.
  • Keep scenes simple and uncluttered, easy to read. Let the human reaction lead.

Don't

  • Horror-style, fear-driven imagery (dark rooms, red sirens, screens-on-faces-in-the-dark).
  • Exaggerated hackers in hoodies and green code rain. We're not a cybersecurity vendor.
  • Overly staged, cheesy stock photos with fake smiles. Nobody holds a laptop like that.
  • Users shown as helpless victims being scolded or embarrassed. We empower, not shame.
  • Overload images with UI mockups, text, or infographic overlays. Let the photo breathe.

10 · Sound
Receipts you can hear.
Five synthesized cues — no asset files, no licensing, no system-tone fatigue. Each one is shorter than 280 ms and earns its keep by signaling state, not decorating it. Sound is on by default — toggle it off in the corner if you need silence.
cue.hover Whisper-quiet sine tick @ 2.2 kHz · 22 ms · gain 0.04. Plays on hover into any interactive element. Throttled to 110 ms.
cue.tap Sine tick @ 1.2 kHz · 40 ms. Plays on every primary & secondary button press.
cue.tick Triangle pop sliding 520 → 380 Hz · 70 ms. Pairs with counter-bump on peer-report increments.
cue.safe Two-note sine rise C5 → E5 · 220 ms. Confirms a safe verdict — quietly positive.
cue.block Sub-bass thunk + low-pass noise tail · 220 ms. Fires when user backs out of a scam page via the danger CTA.
Rules: on by default · master capped at 0.55 · hover only on pointer: fine · auto-mute under prefers-reduced-motion.

# ScamAdviser B2C — Tagline System

One hero line. A family behind it. All sound like the same person talking. Source: `scamadviser-b2c-brand.html` §09.

## Hero Tagline

**Check Before You Trust.**

Sub text: *Don't get scammed. Get ScamAdviser!*

Primary lockup tagline. Used on hero moments, out-of-home, app stores, major product surfaces. The hero line is sentence case; the sub line is the action call.

## Alternate Hero (Casual / Consumer)

**your digital bs/detector.**

Lockup tagline for chill, lowercase, consumer-facing surfaces (social, in-product copy, campaign creative). Never translate — the cadence is the point.

## Family

| Context | Line | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| Hero (primary) | Check Before You Trust. | Hero moments, OOH, app stores, partner-facing |
| Hero sub | Don't get scammed. Get ScamAdviser! | Sub-text paired with the primary hero |
| Hero (casual) | your digital bs/detector. | Consumer/social, in-product, campaign creative |
| Campaign hook | Catch what you'd miss. | Ads, campaigns, social headers |
| Community/peer | Millions of users. One group chat. | Community features, peer trust surfaces |
| Product caption | Paste a link. Get the receipts. | Product UI, onboarding, feature callouts |
| Partner/press | Peer-powered scam detection, backed by AI. | Press releases, partner decks, formal contexts |

## Typewriter Rotation (Hero)

The homepage hero cycles through these headlines:

1. your online BS/detector.
2. trust no link /blindly.
3. sketchy link? /we got you.
4. don't get /played online.
5. be street-smart/online.

## Rules

- One tagline per surface — never stack or combine family lines (except the hero + sub pair).
- The primary hero **Check Before You Trust.** always pairs with the sub line **Don't get scammed. Get ScamAdviser!** when used in full hero lockup.
- The slash `/` in the casual hero (`your digital bs/detector.`) is the brand mark — always render it with the 10deg skew.
- Sentence case for the primary hero and press contexts. Lowercase for casual/consumer.
- "ScamAdviser" is always one word, capital S and A — never "Scam Adviser" or "scamadviser" in copy.

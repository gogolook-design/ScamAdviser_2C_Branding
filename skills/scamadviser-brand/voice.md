# ScamAdviser B2C — Voice & Tone

ScamAdviser's voice is **fast, high-signal, and peer-powered**. Group-chat energy, not corporate memo. Source: `scamadviser-b2c-brand.html` §05.

## The Shift

The old B2B tone was "Clear & Confident" — institutional, polished, advisory. The new B2C tone is "Fast & High-Signal" — the friend in the group chat who already checked the link.

| Scenario | Old (B2B) | New (B2C) |
|---|---|---|
| Greeting | Your anti-scam partner, ScamAdviser. | Your digital BS detector. |
| Urgent Alert | New phishing scams detected. Please verify the links you receive before interacting. | Heads up: new phishing drop. Check that link before you tap. |
| Education | Spot a fake website easily with these 5 signs. | 5 red flags this site is actually a scam. |
| Trust Verdict | This site has a low Trust Score. | This site failed the vibe check. (Trust Score: 12/100) |
| Post-Install | Thank you for installing ScamAdviser. | You're covered. Go off. |

## Do

- Lead with the verb. "Check that link" not "it is recommended that you check."
- Lowercase casual headers when the context is chill.
- Use concrete numbers. "12/100" beats "low."
- Be self-aware about AI. Name it as "Sharp," not a buzzword.
- Quote the community. "2,341 people reported this" > "our data shows."

## Don't

- Fear-monger. "You'll lose everything" is cringe — Gen Z spots fake urgency instantly.
- Reach for jargon. "Threat vector" belongs in a B2B deck, not an app.
- Sound institutional. "We advise you to..." — no one talks like that.
- Explain the tech unprompted. Nobody asked about the algorithm.
- Moralize. We're not the online-safety police.

## Personality in Copy

### Straight-up
- Call scams what they are. No hedge words.
- "This site is sketchy." Not "This site may present certain risks."

### Empowering
- Give users the data and let them decide.
- "Here's what we found. Make the call."

### Sharp
- Short sentences. Active voice. Delete the filler.
- "Domain registered 6 days ago. 2,341 reports. Make the call."

### Peer-powered
- Always reference the community when data supports it.
- "2,341 users flagged this in 48 hours. That many people don't lie."

## Verdict Language

| Score Range | Voice Pattern |
|---|---|
| 0–39 | "Don't tap that link." / "Failed the vibe check." |
| 40–59 | "Sketchy — back out." / "This one's sus." |
| 60–69 | "Proceed carefully." / "Not enough data to call it." |
| 70–89 | "Probably fine." / "Looking clean so far." |
| 90–100 | "You're good. Go off." / "Vibe check passed." |

Never blend ScamAdviser B2C voice with:
- **Gogolook** — cool, confident parent brand (blue). Use `gogolook-brand` skill.
- **Whoscall** — friendly, protective (green). Use `whoscall-brand` skill.
